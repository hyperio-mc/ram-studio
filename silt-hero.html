◈ HOW IT WORKS

Built for people
who pay attention.

01 — LOG

Daily observation prompts

Each morning SILT offers a focus — an edge habitat to check, a sound to listen for. Log what you find in seconds. Species name, count, location, conditions.

02 — TRACK

Build your life list

Every species you record builds your personal life list. Birds, plants, fungi, invertebrates. Track first sightings, count totals, follow what's rare for your patch.

03 — UNDERSTAND

Map your patch

A dot-plot map of your personal territory — where observations cluster, where hotspots emerge, how seasonal patterns shift across years of attention.

Everything a field naturalist needs.

SILT is designed around the rhythms of a serious amateur naturalist. Not a tick-list app. Not a social network. A quiet, personal tool for building knowledge of a place over time.

🌿

Seasonal context engine

SILT knows where you are in the year. Migration peaks, flowering windows, emergence dates. Your daily log is framed by the season — not just the calendar.

📍

Patch intelligence

Your patch learns from your observations. Hotspot clustering, species richness maps, comparison to regional and national data for context on what you're finding.

◎

Life list management

A clean, typographically-focused life list for birds, plants, fungi, and invertebrates. First dates, total counts, rarity indicators, and year lists tracked automatically.

📊

Observer scoring

Your observer score tracks consistency, diversity, and patch depth. Streak tracking, personal bests, category breakdown bars — a quiet reflection of dedicated practice.

◈ DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

Quiet tools for patient observers.

Inspired by the material culture of naturalism — field guides, survey notebooks, Ordnance Survey maps. Sandy warm ground. Ink that reads like pencil on paper.

Spectral serif for species names and headings (editorial field-guide weight). Space Mono for coordinates, timestamps, taxonomy codes. The typographic system that a well-designed field notebook would use.

Sandy warm ground — #EAE3D8

Not white. Not grey. The colour of worn paper, good soil, afternoon light through tent canvas. A background that feels like being outside.

Field olive — #4A5E3A

The dominant accent. Birds, primary actions, active states. Not khaki, not forest — the exact olive of military binoculars and wax jacket collars.

Terra cotta — #C07845

Plants, dates, highlights. The warm counterpart to olive. The colour of terracotta plant pots, autumn bracken, copper beech leaves in October.
