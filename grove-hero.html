Core workflow

Designed for the way researchers actually think.

Not another note app. Grove is structured around sessions, sources, and the connections between them.

◉
Today's Focus
A calm daily dashboard that shows your research sessions, progress on active topics, and captures waiting for routing. Starts each morning fresh.
⊞
Research Workspace
A focused note canvas with your sources alongside your thinking. Highlight key insights, track annotation status, and see related connections emerge.
◈
Knowledge Map
A visual topic graph showing how your research areas relate. See which topics are active, which are connected, and where your thinking clusters.
⊕
Capture Inbox
Drop anything in — links, notes, PDFs, images, quotes. Grove routes them to the right research topic, or lets you triage them manually.
✦
Session Synthesis
At the end of a session, Grove surfaces your key insights, cross-topic connections, and offers to draft a synthesis note you can export or share.
🌿
Warm by design
Cream backgrounds, forest-green accents, and editorial serif typography. Research should feel like a notebook, not a dashboard.
Design decisions

Three choices that define the feel.

Palette
Warm cream, not cold white.
Inspired by Amie's warm editorial aesthetic on godly.website — #F7F3EE cream base with forest green (#3A6B4A) and amber (#C47B30) accents. Signals calm focus over productivity anxiety.
Typography
Lora serif + Inter sans.
Headings use italic Lora for an editorial, notebook feel. Data and UI use Inter. The contrast signals "thinking space" not "task manager".

Layout
Bento grid meets editorial hierarchy.
Inspired by The Lookback and Artefakt on Awwwards — editorial bento cards with generous whitespace, left-accent bars for context, and progressive disclosure of information density.
