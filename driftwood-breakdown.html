The Brief

Nature-dark,
not tech-dark.

Most dark UIs borrow from developer tools: near-black surfaces, neon accents, cold greys. DRIFTWOOD needed something warmer — the dark of a forest at dusk, not a terminal window.

"A journaling app should feel like candlelight, not a cockpit."

#000000
Pure Black
Feels harsh. No depth. Absorbs everything.
#0E1209
Forest Night ✦
Slightly green-shifted. Organic. Breathes.

The 5% green shift in #0E1209 is invisible at a glance but makes the whole app feel alive — like the background has atmosphere, not just absence.

Every hex was tuned away from its "obvious" equivalent. Warm cream instead of white (#FFF), amber instead of orange (#FF7F00), sage instead of green (#00FF00). The goal: a palette you can stare at for an hour without strain.

Chosen ✦
Lora — Serif
"Write every day. Especially then."
Serif creates intimacy. The bracketed strokes of a letter feel like handwriting — intentional, personal, warm. Every product app uses sans-serif. A journal shouldn't.
Inter — Sans-serif
"Write every day. Especially then."
Clean and legible, but optimised for interfaces, not introspection. Feels like reading a dashboard. Good for data — wrong for a diary.

Decision · Mood Tags First

The question before
the blank page.

😌 Calm 🌤 Good 🌿 Grounded 🌧 Heavy ⚡ Charged

Appears on every screen — Today, Write, Timeline, Insights. Selecting your mood is the first micro-interaction of every session.

Why this works:

Most journaling apps open to a blank white editor — cognitively expensive, emotionally cold. Asking "how are you?" first is a micro-ritual. It sets context before you type a word. After 6 months, your mood log becomes a dataset about yourself. That's the hidden value.

Today Screen

The 5 screens,
each earning its place.

Today, Write, Timeline, Insights, Profile. No screen is decorative — each is a different relationship with time: the present (Today), the act (Write), the past (Timeline), the pattern (Insights), the self (Profile).

"Five tabs, five different modes of the same person."
